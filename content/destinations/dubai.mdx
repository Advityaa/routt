## Connectivity

Skip the SIM counter at DXB — the queues are long and the tourist packs are overpriced. Set up an **eSIM before you leave India** and you'll have data the second you land and connect to airport Wi-Fi to activate.

For most first-timers, a regional or UAE eSIM with 5–10GB covers a week of maps, ride-hailing, and WhatsApp comfortably. If you're the type who tethers a laptop or video-calls home daily, get an unlimited plan instead.

## Money

The UAE runs on the **dirham (AED)**. Avoid two classic traps: airport currency counters (terrible rates) and your regular Indian debit card abroad (3–3.5% markup plus flat fees per swipe).

A **zero-markup forex card** loads rupees as dirhams at the real Visa/Mastercard rate. You spend like a local, withdraw cash when needed, and freeze the card from an app if it's lost.

## Getting around

**Careem** is the local ride-hailing app (owned by Uber, but more widely used here) — fares are transparent and you avoid taxi haggling. Uber works too. Download and log in before you go.

For getting between the big sights, the **Dubai Metro** is spotless, cheap, and fully air-conditioned. Buy a **silver Nol card** from any station machine, tap in and out. The Red Line covers the airport, Downtown (Burj Khalifa), Marina, and Expo.

- **Short hops in tourist zones:** Metro or tram
- **Evenings / groups / luggage:** Careem
- **Avoid:** unmarked "limo" drivers who approach you at arrivals

## Eating

Dubai is far more than mall food courts. Eat where the city's South Asian and Emirati communities actually go:

- **Al Ustad Special Kabab** (Bur Dubai) — legendary old-school Iranian kababs
- **Ravi Restaurant** (Satwa) — Pakistani-Punjabi, cheap, beloved by everyone from cabbies to expats
- **Arabian/Emirati:** try a proper machboos or a shawarma from a neighbourhood spot, not the airport

Tap water is technically safe but most people drink bottled — it's cheap. Many restaurants are casual; for dinner-with-a-view spots, book ahead.

If you want the headline experiences (Burj Khalifa "At the Top", desert safari, dhow cruise), pre-book online — it's cheaper than the counter and skips queues.

## Arrival tips

- **Visa:** Most Indian passport holders need a pre-arranged UAE tourist visa — sort it before flying (often bundled with flights/hotels). Carry a printout.
- **At immigration:** Have your hotel address and return ticket handy.
- **Dress:** Smart-casual is fine almost everywhere; cover shoulders and knees at mosques and respect Ramadan timings if you visit then.
- **AC is brutal** — carry a light layer for malls, the Metro, and restaurants.
- **Friday** is the holiday/prayer day; some places open late.
- **Travel insurance:** Cheap for a short Gulf trip and worth it for medical cover — buy a per-trip plan before you go.
