## Connectivity

Thailand is one of the easiest places to stay online — but still set up your **eSIM before you fly** so you're not hunting for the AIS/TrueMove counter at Suvarnabhumi after a red-eye. A 7-day data eSIM is cheap and instant.

Data matters more here than most places: you'll lean on it constantly for Grab, Google Maps, and translation.

## Money

Thailand uses the **baht (THB)**. The smart setup is a **zero-markup forex card** for shops, malls, and bigger restaurants, plus cash for street food, markets, and tuk-tuks.

Withdraw cash from bank ATMs (there's a fixed ~220 THB foreign-withdrawal fee per transaction), so take out a larger amount at once rather than many small withdrawals. Avoid the "Dynamic Currency Conversion" prompt — always choose to be charged **in baht**, not rupees.

## Getting around

**Grab** is your best friend — it's the Uber of Southeast Asia and kills the #1 first-timer problem: tuk-tuk and taxi drivers quoting inflated flat fares. Book on the app, see the price upfront, pay by card or cash.

- **Metered taxis** are fine *if* they use the meter — insist on "meter, please" or just use Grab
- **BTS Skytrain & MRT** are fast, cheap, and skip the traffic — ideal for getting across the city
- **Tuk-tuks** — fun once for the experience, but agree the price first and expect to be overcharged
- **Avoid** any driver offering a cheap "city tour" — it's a gem/tailor-shop commission scam

## Eating

Street food in Bangkok is some of the best eating on earth, and it's safe if you follow simple rules: eat where there's a **queue of locals**, food is **cooked fresh in front of you**, and turnover is high.

- **Pad thai, boat noodles, mango sticky rice, som tam** — start here
- **Chinatown (Yaowarat)** after dark — a street-food wonderland
- **Veg/Jain travellers:** learn "gin jay" (I eat vegetarian); many stalls can do without fish sauce if asked, and there are dedicated jay spots
- Drink **bottled water only**, skip ice from sketchy stalls (sealed-bag ice at proper places is fine)

For day trips (floating markets, Ayutthaya temples, island hops if you head south), pre-booking online is cheaper and smoother than arranging on the day.

## Arrival tips

- **Visa:** Indian passport holders currently enjoy visa-free entry to Thailand for short stays — confirm the latest rules before you fly and carry proof of onward travel + hotel booking.
- **Cash on arrival:** Have a little USD or use a forex-card ATM in the arrivals hall for your first baht.
- **Weather:** Hot and humid year-round — light cotton clothes, but carry a layer for fierce mall AC.
- **Temples:** Cover shoulders and knees; shoes off before entering.
- **Travel insurance:** Get a per-trip plan — medical care is good but you want cover for scooter mishaps and the unexpected.
