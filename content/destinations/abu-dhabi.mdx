## Connectivity

Same playbook as the rest of the UAE: set up your **eSIM before you fly** and skip the airport SIM counter. A UAE or Gulf-regional plan covers maps and ride-hailing easily.

## Money

Abu Dhabi uses the **dirham (AED)**. A **zero-markup forex card** gets you the real rate at hotels, malls, and restaurants; keep a little cash for tips and small purchases.

## Getting around

**Careem** (and Uber) work well here. Distances are larger and more spread out than in Dubai, so ride-hailing is the practical default; taxis are plentiful and metered too.

- **Day trips between sights:** Careem is simplest
- **Airport:** use the app or a metered taxi, not unmarked drivers

## Eating

Abu Dhabi's food scene spans excellent Emirati, Lebanese, and South Asian options.

- **Try:** a proper Lebanese mezze, fresh shawarma, and Emirati dishes like machboos
- The corniche and old-town spots beat the mall chains for value and atmosphere
- Bottled water is the norm and cheap

For the headline experiences — the **Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque**, **Louvre Abu Dhabi**, **Ferrari World**, and **Yas Waterworld** — pre-book online to save time and money.

## Arrival tips

- **Visa:** Most Indian passport holders need a UAE tourist visa — arrange it before flying (it covers the whole UAE, including Dubai).
- **Grand Mosque dress code:** Strict — long sleeves and trousers/long skirts; women cover hair. Abayas can be borrowed on site. No shorts.
- **AC** indoors is strong; carry a layer.
- **Travel insurance:** Quick and cheap for a short Gulf trip — get medical cover sorted.
