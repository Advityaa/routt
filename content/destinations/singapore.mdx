## Connectivity

Singapore has world-class connectivity, but you still want data the moment you land at Changi. Set up an **eSIM before flying** — a small Singapore or regional Asia plan is plenty for a few days of maps and messaging.

## Money

Singapore uses the **Singapore dollar (SGD)** and it's an expensive city, so avoiding the 3%+ markup on a regular Indian card genuinely adds up. Use a **zero-markup forex card** for everything and keep a little cash for hawker stalls.

Contactless is everywhere — you can even tap your forex card directly on MRT gates and buses (SimplyGo) instead of buying a transit card.

## Getting around

The **MRT** is clean, cheap, and reaches almost everywhere a first-timer wants to go — it's the default. **Grab** covers the rest (late nights, luggage, Sentosa runs).

- **MRT/bus:** tap your contactless card straight on the gate
- **Grab:** upfront pricing, no haggling, very reliable
- Taxis are honest and metered, just pricier than Grab

## Eating

Singapore's **hawker centres** are the answer to its high prices — incredible, cheap, multi-ethnic food in one place. This is where locals eat daily.

- **Maxwell, Lau Pa Sat, Old Airport Road** — start here
- **Must-try:** Hainanese chicken rice, char kway teow, chili crab, roti prata
- **Veg/Indian:** Little India has superb, affordable vegetarian options
- Tap water is safe to drink straight from the tap — no need to buy bottled

For Gardens by the Bay, Universal Studios, and the Night Safari, pre-book tickets online to save money and skip queues.

## Arrival tips

- **Visa:** Indian passport holders need a visa for Singapore — arrange it before flying. Also fill the free **SG Arrival Card** online within 3 days of arrival.
- **Strict rules:** No chewing gum imports, no littering, no jaywalking — fines are real. Vaping is banned outright.
- **AC is intense** indoors — carry a layer.
- **Travel insurance:** Short, cheap, and smart for any first trip.
