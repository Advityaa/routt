## Connectivity

Bali's mobile coverage is good in the tourist belt and patchy in the hills, so reliable data matters. Set up an **Indonesia eSIM before you fly** — you'll lean on it for Gojek, Maps, and finding that hidden waterfall.

## Money

Indonesia uses the **rupiah (IDR)** — you'll briefly be a millionaire. Use a **zero-markup forex card** for hotels, restaurants, and bigger shops, and carry cash for warungs (local eateries), markets, and scooter rentals.

Withdraw from **bank ATMs only** (BCA, Mandiri) — avoid the standalone machines and always be charged in rupiah, not rupees.

## Getting around

**Gojek** and **Grab** are the ride apps — both do cars and cheap scooter-taxis, plus food delivery. They save you from the inflated "transport, boss?" street quotes.

- **Short hops:** Gojek/Grab bike (cheap, beats the traffic)
- **Longer trips / groups:** book a car-and-driver for the day — standard in Bali and great value
- **Renting a scooter?** Only with a valid international permit and a helmet — and make sure your insurance covers it (many don't)

## Eating

Eat at **warungs** for authentic, cheap Indonesian food, and you'll spend a fraction of the beach-club prices.

- **Must-try:** nasi goreng, mie goreng, satay, babi guling (if you eat pork), and fresh smoothie bowls
- **Avoid Bali belly:** bottled/filtered water only, including for brushing teeth; be cautious with raw salads and ice at very cheap stalls
- Ubud and Canggu are loaded with great veg/vegan-friendly spots

For temple tours, Nusa Penida day trips, and water sports, pre-book online.

## Arrival tips

- **Visa:** Indian passport holders get **visa-on-arrival** at Denpasar (pay the fee in cash or card) — have it ready. Also complete the online customs declaration.
- **Scooter safety:** Most travel mishaps in Bali are scooter accidents — ride carefully, wear a helmet, and **confirm your insurance covers two-wheelers**.
- **Tourist tax:** A small levy applies — pay it online before arrival.
- **Respect temples:** Wear a sarong (provided at entrances) and cover shoulders.
