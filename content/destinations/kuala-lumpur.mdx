## Connectivity

Coverage in KL is strong. Set up a **Malaysia eSIM before you fly** so Grab and Maps work the moment you land at KLIA — far easier than queuing for a local SIM.

## Money

Malaysia uses the **ringgit (MYR)**. A **zero-markup forex card** handles malls, restaurants, and Grab; carry cash for hawker centres and night markets.

Always choose to be charged in **ringgit**, not rupees, when a card terminal asks.

## Getting around

**Grab** is the default — cheap, upfront pricing, no haggling. KL also has a decent **LRT/MRT/Monorail** network for beating traffic between major points.

- **Grab:** best for door-to-door and late nights
- **Rail:** great for hops between KLCC, Bukit Bintang, and Chinatown
- Avoid unmetered street taxis that refuse the app

## Eating

KL's food is a Malay–Chinese–Indian mashup and it's spectacular.

- **Must-try:** nasi lemak, char kway teow, banana-leaf rice, satay, and roti canai
- **Jalan Alor** night food street is the classic first stop
- **Veg/Indian:** Brickfields (Little India) is a goldmine of affordable vegetarian food
- Bottled water is the norm; food courts and busy hawker stalls are safe bets

For Batu Caves, the Petronas Towers skybridge, and day trips, pre-book online.

## Arrival tips

- **Visa:** Indian passport holders currently enjoy visa-free entry to Malaysia for short stays — confirm the latest rules and complete the free **MDAC** (digital arrival card) online before flying.
- **Onward proof:** Carry your return ticket and hotel booking.
- **Weather:** Hot, humid, with afternoon downpours — pack a compact umbrella and a layer for AC.
- **Travel insurance:** Cheap and worth it for a first trip.
