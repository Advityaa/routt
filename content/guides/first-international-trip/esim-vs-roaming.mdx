This is the decision most first-timers make badly — usually by panic-buying a roaming pack at the departure gate, or by landing with no plan and overpaying for an airport SIM after a long flight.

The honest comparison is simpler than the telecom marketing makes it sound.

## What each one is

**International roaming** is your existing Indian number (Jio, Airtel, Vi) working abroad on a pack you activate before you go. Convenient, because your number just works — calls, SMS, OTPs, data, all on the SIM you already have.

**A travel eSIM** is a separate data plan from a provider like Airalo that you install over Wi-Fi before you fly and switch on when you land. It's a digital SIM, so there's nothing to collect and nothing to swap.

The key thing: these aren't mutually exclusive. The setup that works for almost everyone is **both** — eSIM for data, Indian SIM kept live for OTPs.

## When the eSIM wins (most of the time)

If your trip is more than a couple of days and you'll use maps, ride apps and messaging — which is every first trip — a travel eSIM is usually far cheaper per gigabyte than a roaming pack. You also dodge the airport SIM queue entirely, and you're online the second you land.

There's a destination-specific reason too. In the UAE, WhatsApp and FaceTime *voice and video* calls are blocked on local networks — but on an international travel eSIM, WhatsApp video to home usually still works. For a first-timer who just wants to call family from Dubai, that alone settles it.

## When roaming still makes sense

Roaming isn't always the villain. It wins when:

- Your trip is very short — a day or two — and you mostly need your **number** live, not much data.
- You'd rather not think about a second line at all and your carrier has a genuinely cheap short pack.

For most week-long first trips, though, the eSIM-plus-Indian-SIM combo is cheaper and just as convenient.

## The OTP problem, solved

The worry we hear most: *"If I use an eSIM, how do I get my bank OTPs?"*

You keep your physical Indian SIM in the phone. It stays reachable for calls and SMS — so OTPs arrive normally — while the eSIM carries your data. The one setting to change: turn **mobile data off** on the Indian SIM so it never quietly uses roaming data. Two minutes in settings and you've got the best of both.
