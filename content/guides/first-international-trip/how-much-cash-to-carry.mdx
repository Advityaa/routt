The instinct on a first trip is to carry *too much* cash. A thick stack of dirhams or baht feels safe. In practice it's the opposite — it's the one thing you can't freeze from an app if it goes missing.

So flip the question. You don't need "enough cash for the trip." You need enough for the things that are genuinely cash-only, and a good card for everything else.

## A sensible amount to actually carry

There's no single right number — it depends on the country and how you travel — but a useful rule for a first-timer is **two to three days of small, in-person spending in cash**, topped up from an ATM as you go.

In rough terms, that's often the equivalent of **₹3,000 to ₹8,000** to land with. A street-food-and-metro day in Bangkok burns through very little cash; a day of taxis and tips in Dubai burns more. Withdraw a bit more locally if you need it rather than flying with a brick of notes.

What's the cash actually *for*?

- Taxis and the occasional driver who prefers cash
- Tips
- Small markets, street stalls, temple donations
- A buffer for the rare place that doesn't take cards

Everything else — hotels, restaurants, malls, attraction tickets, your Careem or Grab — goes on a card.

## Put the rest on the right card

This is where the savings live. A normal Indian debit card abroad quietly adds a markup of around 3 to 3.5 percent plus fees; a zero-markup forex card avoids most of that and lets you lock the rate by loading in advance.

## The limits worth knowing

A couple of practical guardrails, not the fine print of the entire FEMA rulebook:

- There are limits on how much foreign currency you can carry out of India, and larger amounts may need to be declared on arrival at your destination. The thresholds vary by country, so check your destination's customs page if you're carrying a lot.
- Withdraw from bank ATMs abroad rather than standalone machines in tourist zones, and always choose to be charged in the **local currency**, not rupees — the "pay in INR" option hides a worse exchange rate.

## The honest version

Carry a little, card the rest, top up locally. That's it. The cash question feels big before a first trip and turns out to be the easy part.
